        <h2>Subjects</h2>
        <div class="content-box">
            <h3>Mathematics</h3>
            <p>Understanding patterns, logic, and numbers. Key areas:</p>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Algebra:</strong>  <div class="content-box"> 
                    <p>Mathematics helps us develop logical reasoning and problem-solving skills. One of the key branches is:</p>
                
                    <h4>Algebra</h4>
                    <p>Algebra is a branch of mathematics dealing with symbols and the rules for manipulating those symbols. It is the unifying thread of almost all of mathematics and includes everything from solving elementary equations to studying abstractions like groups, rings, and fields.</p>
                
                    <h5>Core Topics in Algebra:</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>Variables & Expressions:</strong> Using letters (like x, y) to represent numbers in formulas and expressions.</li>
                        <li><strong>Equations:</strong> Solving for unknowns in expressions like <code>2x + 3 = 7</code>.</li>
                        <li><strong>Functions:</strong> Understanding relationships like <code>f(x) = x²</code>.</li>
                        <li><strong>Polynomials:</strong> Expressions with multiple terms like <code>x² + 2x + 1</code>.</li>
                        <li><strong>Factoring:</strong> Breaking down expressions like <code>x² - 9</code> into <code>(x - 3)(x + 3)</code>.</li>
                        <li><strong>Linear Equations:</strong> Equations that graph as straight lines.</li>
                        <li><strong>Inequalities:</strong> Expressions using <code>&lt;, &gt;, ≤, ≥</code> to compare values.</li>
                    </ul>
                
                    <h5>Why Learn Algebra?</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li>It builds problem-solving skills used in science, engineering, and finance.</li>
                        <li>It’s essential for advanced mathematics and standardized tests.</li>
                        <li>It teaches abstract thinking — how to generalize problems and solutions.</li>
                    </ul>
                
                    <p><strong>Example Problem:</strong> Solve <code>3x - 5 = 16</code> → <code>x = 7</code></p>
                </div>
                </li>
                <li><strong>Geometry:</strong><div class="content-box"> 
                    <p>Geometry is a branch of mathematics that deals with shapes, sizes, and properties of space. It’s the study of points, lines, surfaces, and solids. Geometry has many applications in architecture, engineering, astronomy, and more.</p>
                
                    <h5>Core Topics in Geometry:</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>Points and Lines:</strong> Basic building blocks of geometry. Points are locations in space, and lines connect points.</li>
                        <li><strong>Angles:</strong> Formed by two intersecting lines. Types of angles include acute, obtuse, right, and straight.</li>
                        <li><strong>Triangles:</strong> Three-sided polygons. Types include equilateral, isosceles, and scalene triangles.</li>
                        <li><strong>Circles:</strong> Study of properties like radius, diameter, circumference, and area.</li>
                        <li><strong>Polygons:</strong> Closed shapes with more than three sides. Examples: squares, rectangles, pentagons.</li>
                        <li><strong>Area and Perimeter:</strong> How to calculate the area and perimeter of different shapes like squares, rectangles, triangles, and circles.</li>
                        <li><strong>Volume and Surface Area:</strong> Calculating the volume and surface area of 3D shapes like cubes, spheres, and cylinders.</li>
                    </ul>
                
                    <h5>Why Learn Geometry?</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li>Helps in understanding the spatial relationships and measurements of objects.</li>
                        <li>Used in real-life applications such as architecture, engineering, and art.</li>
                        <li>Improves logical thinking and problem-solving skills.</li>
                    </ul>
                
                    <p><strong>Example Problem:</strong> Calculate the area of a rectangle with length 5 cm and width 3 cm. The area is <code>length × width = 5 × 3 = 15 cm²</code>.</p>
                </div>
                </li>
                <li><strong>Calculus:</strong><div class="content-box"> 
                    <p>Calculus is a branch of mathematics that studies continuous change. It has two main components: <strong>differential calculus</strong>, which deals with the rate of change, and <strong>integral calculus</strong>, which focuses on the accumulation of quantities. Calculus is fundamental in understanding motion, growth, and many phenomena in physics, economics, and engineering.</p>
                
                    <h5>Core Topics in Calculus:</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>Limits:</strong> Understanding how a function behaves as it approaches a specific point.</li>
                        <li><strong>Derivatives:</strong> Measures the rate of change of a function. The derivative of a function represents the slope of the tangent line at any point on the curve.</li>
                        <li><strong>Integrals:</strong> The process of finding the area under a curve. It can be thought of as the accumulation of quantities such as distance or area.</li>
                        <li><strong>Chain Rule:</strong> A rule for differentiating composite functions.</li>
                        <li><strong>Product and Quotient Rules:</strong> Methods for differentiating products and quotients of functions.</li>
                        <li><strong>Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:</strong> Links the concept of differentiation with integration, showing that they are essentially inverse operations.</li>
                    </ul>
                
                    <h5>Why Learn Calculus?</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li>Essential for solving problems in physics, engineering, economics, and many other fields.</li>
                        <li>Helps in understanding changes and motion in the real world.</li>
                        <li>Improves problem-solving skills and logical thinking.</li>
                        <li>Provides the foundation for advanced topics in mathematics and science.</li>
                    </ul>
                
                    <p><strong>Example Problem:</strong> Find the derivative of the function <code>f(x) = 3x² + 5x + 2</code>. The derivative, <code>f'(x) = 6x + 5</code>.</p>
                </div>
                </li>
            </ul>
        </div>
